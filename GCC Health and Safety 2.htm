Health and Safety

Occupational health and safety is.....

  • knowing the risks in your work activities
  • putting in place means to control those risks so that you are not harmed
  • preventing harm to others affected by our work – service users, visitors and contractors for example
  • providing support early to help reduce sickness absence
  • promoting health and wellbeing

Typical risks might include:

  • lone working
  • use of various items of equipment
  • driving at work
    or
  • aggression from members of the public

The way risks are controlled includes:

  • providing training and supervision
  • maintaining equipment
  • adopting safe working systems
    or
  • using materials that are not hazardous
  • health surveillance
  • supporting mangers and staff

At the heart of all this is risk assessment

Your line manager should share with you the key findings from risk assessments for your job

See the SHE webpages for more information

Responsibility

We can stay safe by:

  • following workplace rules;
  • observing safety signs;
  • having tidy workplaces;
  • applying what we have learned on a course;
  • using equipment or machinery correctly;
  • reporting defects or hazards;
  • seeking advice
    and
  • communicating effectively.
