Safeguarding Adults

Definition of a Vulnerable Adult as stated by No Secrets 2000
A person aged 18 and over, who is or may be in need of community care services by reason of mental or other disability, age or illness; and who is or may be unable to take care of him or herself, or unable to protect him or herself against significant harm or exploitation

Definition of Abuse
Abuse is a violation of an individual’s human and civil rights by any other person or persons.

  • Abuse may consist of a single act or repeated acts.
  • It may involve pressure with regard to consent.
  • It may be an act of neglect or an omission to act.
  • It can cause development to be impaired and deterioration which could have been avoided.
  • Abuse can occur in any relationship and may result in significant harm to or exploitation of, the person subjected to it.         

Types of Abuse

  1. Physical – Hitting, slapping, misuse of medication
  2. Psychological – Harassment, verbal abuse, isolation & coercion
  3. Financial – Fraud, theft, misuse of property & possessions
  4. Neglect (can now include serious self-neglect) – ignoring medical and physical needs, failure to access appropriate health & social care facilities
  5. Sexual – rape, sexual assaults and acts without consent
  6. Institutional - can occur in a care home, nursing home, acute hospital or in-patient setting.
  7. Discrimination – e.g. racist, ageist, sexist slurs making jokes about someone’s condition

Mental Capacity Act
The Mental Capacity Act 2005 is a vitally important piece of legislation, and one that will make a real difference to the lives of people who may lack mental capacity. It will empower people to make decisions for themselves wherever possible, and protect people who lack capacity by providing a flexible framework that places individuals at the very heart of the decision-making process.
